About Wheeler Diagrams

Wheeler diagrams (chronostratigraphic charts) are essential tools in sequence stratigraphy for visualizing the temporal relationships between stratigraphic units. Unlike traditional cross-sections that show spatial relationships, Wheeler diagrams plot relative geologic time on the vertical axis against distance on the horizontal axis.

Key concepts you will practice:

  • Stratal terminations: Onlap, downlap, toplap, and truncation patterns
  • Systems tracts: HST, TST, LST, FSST identification and mapping
  • Key surfaces: Sequence boundaries (SB), Maximum Flooding Surfaces (MFS), Transgressive Surfaces (TS)
  • Hiatuses and lacunae: Identifying gaps in the stratigraphic record
  • Accommodation/Sediment supply: Understanding A/S ratios through stratal patterns
